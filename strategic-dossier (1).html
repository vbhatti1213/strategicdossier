Messaging & Narrative Systems

Narrative Repositioning at Scale —
Cashco Financial

An organization without a brand operating in a category defined by stigma. The challenge was not building a campaign — it was building the architecture that would let every touchpoint carry the same message.

Stakeholder Environment

60+ branches across Alberta. Frontline staff navigating financial shame daily. Executive team mid-$50M capital raise. Board requiring governance-level narrative clarity. 150K+ clients — many newcomers, first-generation Canadians, and underbanked individuals.

System Diagnosis

No brand infrastructure. Communications reactive and inconsistent across 60+ locations. Transactional language reinforcing the stigma the organization needed to dismantle. No internal framework for staff to address financial shame constructively.

Approach

Built full narrative architecture anchored in a BHAG: End Financial Exclusion — with purpose defined as putting one million Canadians on a pathway to financial health. Designed a From/To language framework across credit, debt, and financial behaviour. Built an Empathy Framework for frontline staff. Launched financial literacy programs including a customized Wealthing Like Rabbits and The Bunny's Talk Money Book (ages 5–8).

Decision Points

Repositioned core product from "payday loan" to "wage advance" — a language shift with measurable behaviour implications. Replaced the 50-30-20 budget with an 80-10-10 model built for real financial starting points. Built a credit model decoupled from income correlation.

Community & Partnership Development

Province-Wide Stakeholder Network —
Alberta First Responders Association

A mission-critical organization with no marketing infrastructure, serving a community culturally resistant to help-seeking. The work required building a stakeholder ecosystem — not a campaign — that could normalize mental health conversations across an entire provincial sector.

Stakeholder Environment

Alberta-wide — police, fire, EMS, corrections. Multiple municipalities, health authorities, and sport organizations. A culture that prizes stoicism and resists institutional messaging. Volunteer CMO — zero budget, full mandate.

System Diagnosis

No brand, no partnerships, no system. General awareness campaigns failing to penetrate a community that distrusts institutional messaging. Peer voice was the only credible channel — with no infrastructure to activate it.

Approach

Built brand and communications strategy from near-zero. Anchored positioning on psychological fitness as professional standard — not crisis intervention. Designed peer-first communications: every campaign led with first responder voices, not clinicians or executives. Operational vocabulary throughout — strength framing, not vulnerability framing. Developed a provincial Crisis Communications Playbook.

Decision Points

Flipped the standard mental health communications frame — leading with reframing before any ask. Tone shift: "It's okay to ask for help" → "Seeking support keeps you in the job." Built the province-wide gala as a stakeholder alignment and fundraising vehicle — scaled from 75 to 300 tickets sold in 4 weeks.

Essential Services Transition —
COVID-19 Business Continuity

When provincial lockdowns were declared, Cashco Financial had 72 hours to migrate 60+ essential service locations to remote-only operations — without a service gap, without client panic, and without a communications playbook.

Stakeholder Environment

Executive team, 60+ branch managers across Alberta and BC, frontline staff navigating personal fear alongside operational change, and a vulnerable client base with limited digital access. Government directives arriving province by province, in real time.

Coordination Challenge

No remote service infrastructure. Low-digital-literacy client base requiring calm, plain-language guidance. Staff needing safety assurance and role clarity simultaneously. No communications templates — everything built under pressure.

Approach

Supported province-wide communications strategy — internal and external. Built client-facing updates translating government directives into plain language. Developed staff briefings addressing safety and continuity simultaneously. Coordinated rollout province by province as directives evolved.

Decision Points

Separated external calm from internal complexity — client communications simple and reassuring, internal briefings operationally detailed. Established daily leadership update cadence as guidance shifted, preventing fragmentation across 60+ locations.
